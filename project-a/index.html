Glacien

Scientific Name:

Lacustrocetus Stellaris

Discovered by / at:

Shepherd from neighbouring village; Iceland 2025

Family:

Cetaceans

Habitat:

Desolate lakes in volcanic highlands, formed by ancient eruptions and later preserved by glacial ice.

Appearance:

Glacien is a deep navy creature whose structure resembles a dolphin, although it is more commonly observed as a dark, shifting shadow underwater. Thin, luminous rings orbit around its head. People regard them as divine beings.

Key Characteristics:

  • Naturally playful
  • Deeply cautious - rarely appear in open air, only to be sighted by the lucky ones
  • Unexplainable and unjustifiable obsession with stars

What we know so far about Glacien The existence of Glacien was confirmed only recently, though the species itself is believed to be ancient. It spends most of its time gliding through the depths or playing in solitude, and only surfaces when it senses complete stillness and safety or when a star is present.

Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Interaction is encouraged with caution and respect. When hovering the mouse (star) on the canvas, the Glacien will swim after the mouse, like a predator hunting for its prey. Once it reaches the star, it will leap out of the water and balance it on its head.
